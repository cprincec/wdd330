Master Mobile UX

This section contains my notes from Master UX lecture

Important Principles

Rewiring the brain for mobile first

  • The use of mobile devices in accessing the internet has skyrocketted over the years. and calls for focus in making sure mobile users have a good experience with the site to encourage site traffic. This increase in mobile usage highlights the need/importance of mobile-first approach to web development.
  • Reponsive retrofitting: Taking what you have and making it work on mobile. Compressing site content initially developed for desktop to fit into phone screens.
  • There are more upsides to mobile-first than downsides.

Understanding Users

  • Its important to know users so you can best get a feel of what they want. "....a lot of times, people don't know what they want until you show it to them." Steve jobs.
  • provide users with what they want as soon as possible. Make interactions smooth and seamless.
  • Make key items within easy click range.

Primary controls

  • Primary control buttons eg Menu button; should be placed at easily reachable area such as the bottom of the screen.
  • Make primary control icons explicit ie easily identifiable

Mobile friendly forms

  • Make forms simple. only require needed information in forms.
  • Make field (inputs, checkboxes etc) easily clickable by increasing with or padding.
  • Allow user to hide password if they need to for easy entry
  • Spacing
  • Provide user with proper keyboard for the required input. Eg for inputs of phone numbers, show only the numbers keyboard.
  • Link phone number so users can easily put call across by a simgle tap instead of copy and paste.

Advertising

  • Make research before advertising to understand how users use the device and more.
  • Avoid autoplay video, audio, pop-ups
  • Avoid distracting ad placements.
  • Make ads smaller and simple. Use reveal on click adds.
  • Content before ads.
